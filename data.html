Introduction

Saul Bass (May 8, 1920 â€” April 25, 1996) was a graphic designer and filmmaker, perhaps best known for his design of film posters and title sequences. For this project, I researched the movies that Saul Bass have created the opening title sequences for. From watching the movies I have collected data on the manners of death that different movies directors used.

Data Visualisation

I have tried various types of data visualisation, the first graph that I created was a donut graph that used a condensed version of my dataset as my original dataset had too many different variables that made it difficult to manage. With the same dataset, I created a line graph which did not work well because the condensed dataset is not arranged by date. I felt like it was lacking so I created another p5.js sketch where this time it uses multiple line graphs to describe a manner of death. Link to my data visualisation.

Findings

There was an eight per cent death rate for movie characters dying from burns in 1957 and 1960, six per cent death rate from cannon attacks in 1957, one per cent death rate from drowning in 199, ten per cent death rate from exhaustion in 1960 and five per cent death rate from explosions in 1956 and 1995.

A five per cent death from falls in 1958-1960, forty-six per cent death rate from gun wounds in 1956 to 1957, 1959 and 1963 to 1955, one per cent death rate from heart attacks in 1955, sixteen per cent death rate from impact in 1956 to 1957, 1960 and 1955, one per cent death rate from poisoning in 1958, nineteen per cent death rate from stab wounds in 1957, 1960, 1990 and 1955 and four per cent death rate from strangulation in 1957 and 1991 to 1995.

Insights

From the data calculations that I made, characters dying from their guns wounds were the most in comparison with the other manners of deaths in my dataset. While characters dying from poisoning and drowning is an uncommon method of death as it happened once which is the least out of all the 12 manners of death in my dataset.

Conclusion

From the findings that I made I realised that the movies directed by Alfred Hitchock seem to involve a lot of death from falling. The surprising thing was that after rewatching the movie Psycho that was released in 1960, the movie did not show as much killing as I remembered from my memory but it was still the scariest because of Hitchock directing and soundtrack choice.
